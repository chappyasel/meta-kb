## Jeff Clune

**Role:** Professor, University of British Columbia; Research Scientist (Meta AI, previously OpenAI)
**Focus:** Open-endedness, AI-Generating Algorithms (AI-GAs), self-improving agents, neuroevolution

## Who He Is

Jeff Clune studies how to build AI systems that improve themselves and generate novel capabilities without human intervention. His core thesis, formalized in the AI-Generating Algorithms (AI-GA) framework, is that the path to general AI runs through automated generation of training environments, agents, and learning algorithms rather than hand-crafted designs. He has held research positions at OpenAI and Meta AI alongside his academic role at UBC.

## Key Contributions

**AI-Generating Algorithms (AI-GAs).** Clune's 2019 paper argues that three components must be automated together: learning algorithms, the environments agents are trained in, and the agent architectures themselves. The paper frames open-ended evolution as the mechanism that could drive all three. This framing has become a touchstone for researchers working on meta-learning and self-improving systems.

**Automated Design of Agentic Systems (ADAS).** Co-authored with Shengran Hu and Cong Lu, ADAS introduces Meta Agent Search, a meta-agent that iteratively programs new agent designs in code and evaluates them against benchmarks. The archive of discovered agents accumulates across iterations, and novel designs are generated by sampling prior agents and prompting a foundation model to produce variants. The paper was accepted at ICLR 2025 and won Outstanding Paper at the NeurIPS 2024 Open-World Agent Workshop. The ADAS repository has ~1,500 GitHub stars. [Source](../raw/repos/shengranhu-adas.md)

**Darwin Gödel Machine (DGM).** Co-authored with Jenny Zhang, Shengran Hu, Cong Lu, and Robert Lange, the DGM extends self-improvement to the codebase level: the agent modifies its own code, validates changes empirically on coding benchmarks, and maintains an archive of agent variants that forms a tree of explored designs. The system improved SWE-bench performance from 20.0% to 50.0% and Polyglot from 14.2% to 30.7% through automated self-modification, with safety precautions including sandboxing and human oversight. These numbers are self-reported in the paper (arXiv 2505.22954). [Source](../raw/papers/zhang-darwin-godel-machine-open-ended-evolution-of-self.md)

**Hyperagents (DGM-H).** An extension of DGM where both the task-solving behavior and the self-improvement procedure itself are editable. This enables what the authors call metacognitive self-modification. Results across coding, paper review, robotics reward design, and Olympiad-level math show consistent improvements over DGM. Work done in collaboration with Meta AI researchers including Jakob Foerster and Minqi Jiang. [Source](../raw/tweets/jennyzhangzt-introducing-hyperagents-an-ai-system-that-not-onl.md)

**Earlier work.** Clune's earlier career produced significant results in neuroevolution (NEAT extensions), quality-diversity algorithms (MAP-Elites lineage), and deep neural network visualization. The Voyager paper (autonomous Minecraft agent with an ever-growing skill library) sits in an adjacent space; Clune's open-endedness framing influenced that line of work.

## Relationship to Key Concepts

His work sits at the intersection of several active research threads covered in this knowledge base. The DGM directly instantiates the [Self-Improving Agent](../concepts/self-improving-agent.md) pattern. ADAS is a concrete implementation of [Meta-Agent](../concepts/meta-agent.md) design search. The archive-based exploration in both DGM and ADAS relates to [Automatic Curriculum](../concepts/automatic-curriculum.md) construction. The skill accumulation design in Voyager (which his lab influenced) connects to [Agent Skills](../concepts/agent-skills.md) and [Procedural Memory](../concepts/procedural-memory.md).

The [Darwin Gödel Machine](../projects/darwin-godel-machine.md) and [HyperAgents](../projects/hyperagents.md) are the most direct project implementations of his current research direction.

## What He's Arguing

Across his recent papers, Clune consistently makes one bet: empirical validation can substitute for provable correctness when the search space is large enough and the benchmark signal is reliable. The Gödel machine required proofs; DGM replaces proofs with benchmark scores. This is pragmatic but shifts the question to benchmark validity — systems optimized against coding benchmarks may not generalize to domains where such tight feedback loops don't exist.
