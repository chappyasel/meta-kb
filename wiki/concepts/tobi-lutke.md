## Tobi Lütke

Tobi Lütke is the CEO and co-founder of Shopify, the e-commerce platform he built starting in 2004 after growing frustrated with existing online store software while trying to sell snowboards. He wrote the original Liquid templating engine that still powers every Shopify storefront.

Within the AI and agent systems space, Lütke gained attention for his public advocacy of treating AI capability as a core competency rather than a productivity tool. He has written internally at Shopify about expectations that employees use AI agents aggressively in their workflows, framing AI fluency as a baseline job requirement rather than optional upskilling. This framing influenced broader conversations about how engineering organizations should integrate agentic AI into software development.

His most cited contribution to the technical discourse came from applying Andrej Karpathy's autoresearch loop to Liquid, the template engine he originally authored two decades prior. Lütke ran 120 automated experiments overnight, producing 93 commits that yielded 53% faster rendering with 61% fewer memory allocations, with all 974 unit tests passing. He has also publicly endorsed [GEPA](../projects/gepa.md), describing it as "severely under hyped in the AI context engineering world," which drew attention to the prompt optimization framework. [Source](../../raw/repos/gepa-ai-gepa.md)

Lütke is not an AI researcher. His influence in this space comes from his position as a practitioner-executive publicly running and reporting on agent-driven experiments at production scale.
