Introducing the AI Transformation Model

How AI native are you? Every company needs AI, but many lack a clear starting point or scaling path. This model shows how to go from manual, fragmented work to AI-powered operations. It mirrors real journeys tracking what changes, expected impact, and the role Notion can play. Every framework has flaws, and no company’s journey is identical to another’s, but we’ve found this model useful for plotting a course.

Created in partnership with Ben Levick (Head of AI & Ops @ Ramp), Geoffrey Litt (Author, MIT researcher, Engineer @ Notion), and many others.

Quick Guide

Companies operate at multiple levels. Teams and workflows mature independently. Engineering may automate processes (Level 3), while marketing is at Level 2.

Levels build on each other, they don't replace each other. Successful companies continue using tools from earlier levels while adding new capabilities.

Context is the foundation. Consolidating tools and connecting data sources unlocks every level above Level 1. The more context AI has, the more value it delivers.

Not everyone needs to be an AI builder. Typically 10-20% of employees create agents that benefit the whole team. Most people will use and approve what's built.

Start where you are. If you're at Level 1 using generic AI chatbots, that's fine. Connect that usage to company context (Level 2) before jumping to automation.

Use this model to align and assess. It becomes a shared language to identify where you are today and where you want to go.
