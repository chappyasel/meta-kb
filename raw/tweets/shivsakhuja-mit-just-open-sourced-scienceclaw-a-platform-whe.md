## Tweet by @shivsakhuja

MIT just open-sourced ScienceClaw — a platform where AI agent swarms self-coordinate to run scientific experiments across institutions.

The agents don't just execute tasks. They evolve. They figure out which of hundreds of scientific tools to use, divide the work, and exploit each other's discoveries.

This is the most ambitious multi-agent system I've seen in the wild. Not "agent A hands off to agent B" — actual swarm intelligence where agents crowdsource discovery across labs worldwide.

What makes this different from the typical multi-agent demo:

1. Scale — this isn't 3 agents on one task. It's an open platform where any institution can contribute agents.

2. Adaptive — the agents adapt their strategies based on what other agents have discovered. The swarm gets smarter as it grows.

3. Tool use — hundreds of scientific tools, and the agents decide which ones to use based on the problem. No human pre-selects the toolkit.

There are wild applications of this concept (even outside research).

I think we're going to see an explosion of agent swarms in the next 6-12 months.
